Passion and Aim

I have always been passionate about working with children, first as a teacher and now as a counsellor. I also enjoy counselling women of all ages, and I have a desire to work with teen mothers.

My aim is to get to know you as an individual and to explore your world in a way that suits  your personality and sits comfortably with you. I believe that a positive therapeutic relationship is important if you are to achieve your goals. I endeavour to listen with sensitivity, hearing your story and using a variety of therapeutic techniques to help you achieve your goals.

I am a Christian, so I am happy to include Biblical worldview throughout sessions, however I will only do this if you desire it.

Services

Children's Issues

  • Anxiety
  • Depression
  • Bullying issues
  • High functioning autism
  • ADHD
  • Friendship issues
  • Trauma
  • Abuse issues
  • Grief
  • ASD strengths and challenges

I generally use Play Therapy and Expressive Therapies when counselling children. Techniques include the use of drawing, stories, role-play, sand-tray, games, play-doh, imagery and more.

I have experience working with Autism Spectrum Disorder, and I am able to use Cognitive Behavioural techniques if required.

Women's Issues

  • Anxiety
  • Depression
  • Relationship issues
  • Self-esteem
  • Trauma
  • Abuse issues
  • Personal issues
  • Grief
  • Parenting

I use a range of therapies depending on the desires of the client and on the issues being presented. I engage in a conversational manner, recognising you as the expert in your world. Sometimes talk therapy is all that is required. At other times I might use Cognitive Behavioural Therapy techniques or Expressive Therapies.

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy can be helpful in dealing with behaviours and thought patterns that are not helpful in everyday life. Expressive Therapies include sand tray, drawing, picture cards, imaginative techniques and role play.
